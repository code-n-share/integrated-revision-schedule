📖 Key Terms
Health
A person's physical and mental wellbeing.
Nutrition
One of the characteristics of all living things; obtaining biomolecules from food.
Biomolecules
Chemical molecules that make up the cells of the body (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, etc.).
Balanced Diet
A diet that provides all biomolecules, minerals and water in the correct quantities.
Micro-organism
A very small living thing — bacteria, virus, or fungi.
Pathogen
A micro-organism that causes disease; also called a germ.
Bacteria
Single-celled micro-organisms; the most common organisms on Earth.
Virus
A small amount of genetic material (e.g. DNA) surrounded by protein.
Fungi
Micro-organisms that cannot produce their own food; they feed on dead material.
Vaccine
A medical treatment made from a pathogen that protects the body from a disease.
Antibiotic
A chemical produced by bacteria and fungi that attacks other bacteria and fungi.
❤️ Health
Health
Health is a person's physical and mental wellbeing.
💡 Health is affected by: inherited factors, environmental factors, and disease caused by harmful organisms.
🧬 Inherited Factors

Human characteristics are controlled by genes found on chromosomes in the nucleus of the body's cells. Mutations (changes) in a gene or chromosome, inherited from one or both parents, can affect a person's health.

Health Conditions Caused by Mutations
Condition Cause Effects
Haemophilia Mutation in the gene for a protein that controls blood clotting Blood does not clot correctly, causing excess bleeding.
Cancer Mutation of a gene that causes cells to divide uncontrollably A mass of cells called a tumour forms.
Down syndrome An extra copy of chromosome number 21 Physical and mental development are affected.

🌍 Environmental Factors

Cancer is not an inherited disease, but gene mutations that increase the risk of developing some cancers (e.g. breast cancer) can be passed from parents to offspring. Cancer-causing mutations can also happen due to environmental factors.

🚬
Smoking
Smokers are at greater risk than non-smokers of developing lung cancer.
☀️
UV Radiation
Exposure to the Sun (or sunbeds) increases the risk of skin cancer, as UV rays in sunlight damage the DNA of skin cells.
🍬
Type 2 Diabetes
Eating too much sugar and fat leads to increased thirst, urination, tiredness and weight loss.
💔
Heart Disease
High blood pressure and high cholesterol caused by eating food with too much salt and saturated fat; also linked to smoking.
🍺
Liver Disease
Obesity caused by eating food with too much sugar and fat; consuming too much alcohol. Effects include yellow skin/eyes and abdominal pain.
💡
Eureka – Marie Curie (1867–1934)
Marie Curie pioneered the first ever trials of radiation therapy to treat cancer. She won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1903 and again in 1911 for her research in radioactivity. Curie died from a disease of the bone marrow brought on by overexposure to radiation.

🥗 Nutrition & Balanced Diet
Nutrition
Nutrition is one of the characteristics of all living things. The cells that make up the body are made from biomolecules. Humans must get biomolecules from their diet, i.e. the food they eat.
Balanced Diet
A balanced diet is a diet that provides a person with biomolecules, minerals and water in the correct quantities.
Name Role in the Diet Sources
Carbohydrates — Starch Slow supply of energy Potatoes, bread, pasta, rice
Carbohydrates — Sugar Fast supply of energy Fruit, sweets
Carbohydrates — Fibre Prevent constipation Brown bread, fruit, vegetables, cereals
Proteins Growth and repair of cells; make muscle, hair and enzymes Lean meat, fish, pulses, dairy products
Fats Provide a store of energy; insulate and protect internal organs Meat, dairy products, chocolate
Vitamins — Vitamin C Keeps teeth, gums, joints and immune system healthy Fresh fruit and vegetables
Vitamins — Vitamin D Helps build strong bones Dairy products
Minerals — Iron Builds biomolecules, e.g. haemoglobin in red blood cells Lean meat, fresh fruit, vegetables
Minerals — Calcium Assists chemical reactions in cells, e.g. transmitting nerve impulses Dairy products
Water Acts as a solvent; transports materials in blood; removes waste; regulates temperature Water, other liquids, fruit, vegetables

🔬 Experimental Investigation 10.1
How can we investigate if starch is present in food?
⚠️ Safety: Do not eat any of the food being tested.
Equipment

4 test tubes · Dropper · Test tube rack · Iodine solution

Suggested Substances to Test

Water (control) · Bread · Milk · Vegetable oil

Method
  1. Label four test tubes: 'Water (control)', 'Bread', 'Milk', 'Vegetable oil'.
  2. Add 2 cm³ of water to the test tube labelled 'Water (control)' and then add 2 cm³ of the correct food to the appropriately labelled test tube.
  3. Add 2–3 drops of iodine to each test tube. Swirl carefully for 5–10 seconds.
  4. Stand all four test tubes in a test tube rack and observe any colour changes. A positive test for starch will show a colour change to blue-black.
  5. Record your results.
🔬 Experimental Investigation 10.2
How can we investigate if protein is present in food?
⚠️ Safety: Do not eat any of the food being tested.
Equipment

4 test tubes · Dropper · Test tube rack · Biuret reagent solution

Suggested Substances to Test

Water (control) · Bread · Milk · Vegetable oil

Method
  1. Label four test tubes: 'Water (control)', 'Bread', 'Milk', 'Vegetable oil'.
  2. Add 2 cm³ of water to the test tube labelled 'Water (control)' and then add 2 cm³ of the correct food to the appropriately labelled test tube.
  3. Add 2 cm³ of Biuret reagent solution to each test tube. Swirl carefully for 5–10 seconds.
  4. Stand all four test tubes in a test tube rack for approximately 5 minutes. Observe any colour changes. A positive test for protein will show a colour change to purple.
  5. Record your results.
🔬 Experimental Investigation 10.3
How can we investigate if fat is present in food?
⚠️ Safety: Do not eat any of the food being tested.
Equipment

Brown paper · Dropper

Suggested Substances to Test

Water (control) · Bread · Milk · Vegetable oil

Method
  1. Label four pieces of brown paper: 'Water (control)', 'Bread', 'Milk', 'Vegetable oil'.
  2. Add a small drop of water to the piece of paper labelled 'Water (control)' and then rub or add small amounts of the food to the appropriately labelled piece of brown paper.
  3. Stand all four pieces of paper on the lab bench for approximately 15 minutes.
  4. Hold up all four pieces of paper to the light. A positive test for fat will allow light to pass through the paper (translucent).
  5. Record your results in a table.

🥦 The Food Pyramid & Energy from Food

The food pyramid can be used to help people plan a balanced diet. To get the correct quantity of biomolecules, vitamins and minerals, a person should eat the most servings from the bottom of the pyramid and only small amounts from the top.

🇮🇪 Ireland's Healthy Food for Life – Food Pyramid

TOP
Foods & drinks high in fat, sugar and salt – Maximum once or twice a week
Fats
Fats, spreads and oils – Very small amounts
Protein
Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, beans and nuts – 2 servings daily
Dairy
Milk, yogurt and cheese – 3 servings daily
Cereals
Wholemeal cereals, breads, potatoes, pasta and rice – 3–5 servings daily (wholegrain best)
Veg & Fruit
Vegetables, salad and fruit – 5–7 servings daily
Factors Affecting Diet
  • Age: young people still growing need more food than adults
  • Sex: males generally need more food than females
  • Genetics: people who are physically larger need more food
  • Health: people who are ill may lose their appetite
  • Physical activity: people with active lifestyles need more food
Energy from Food

The unit of energy is the joule (J). The energy in food is measured in thousands of joules, called kilojoules (kJ). Energy values are given on food packaging. If a person consumes more energy than their body needs, it will be stored as fat, potentially leading to obesity and increased risk of stroke and heart disease.

🦠 Micro-organisms
Micro-organisms
Micro-organisms are very small living things. The three types are: bacteria, viruses, and fungi.
💡 Any micro-organism that causes disease is called a pathogen — or more commonly, a germ.
Bacteria

Bacteria are the most common organisms on Earth. All bacteria are made of only one cell and can reproduce asexually very quickly. Bacteria need food, water and a suitable temperature and pH to survive and reproduce. The perfect conditions for some bacteria are provided by the human body.

🦠
Diseases Caused by Bacteria
Food poisoning · Cholera · Tuberculosis · Tetanus · Pneumonia · Tooth and gum decay
✅
Beneficial Bacteria
Bacteria in our large intestine make vitamins B and K and stop other, more harmful bacteria from growing there.
Also used in dairy products, baking, water treatment and more!
Viruses
Virus
A virus is a small amount of genetic material, e.g. DNA, surrounded by protein. A virus can only reproduce by entering the cells of another organism. Any organism that relies on another organism to live and does harm to that organism is called a parasite.
🧫
Diseases Caused by Viruses
Ebola · COVID-19 · AIDS · The common cold · Influenza · Cold sores · Measles · Mumps · Chickenpox
Fungi
Fungi
Fungi cannot produce their own food, as they do not contain chlorophyll. Instead, they must feed on dead plant and animal material or on parts of other organisms, e.g. dead human skin cells.
🍄
Diseases Caused by Fungi
Athlete's foot · Ringworm

🛡️ Prevention & Treatment of Disease

Most disease-causing micro-organisms are present in the air we breathe or on surfaces we touch. Some pathogens enter the body in undercooked food or contaminated water.

Reducing Chances of Becoming Ill
  • Practise good hygiene — wash your hands with warm water and soap for at least 20 seconds or use hand sanitiser.
  • Practise good coughing etiquette — use a tissue and dispose of it safely.
  • Social distance when necessary — do not go to school, college or work if you are displaying symptoms.
  • Cook food properly.
  • Make sure drinking water is clean.
  • Get adequate sleep and exercise and reduce stress and anxiety.
🔬 Experimental Investigation 10.4
How can we investigate if micro-organisms are present on the laboratory bench?
⚠️ Safety: Keep the bags containing micro-organisms sealed. Your teacher will sterilise the bags before disposing of them.
Equipment

Disinfectant · Chopping board · Sterile gloves · Fresh wholegrain bread · Plastic droppers · Sterilised water · Sandwich bag · Incubator · Magnifying glass

Method
  1. Disinfect a chopping board using alcohol or disinfectant.
  2. Wearing sterile gloves, place a slice of bread on the chopping board.
  3. Drop 5–10 cm³ of sterilised water onto the upper surface of the slice of bread.
  4. Carefully pick up the slice of bread and place the moist upper surface in contact with the non-sterilised laboratory bench for 5–10 seconds.
  5. Place the slice of bread in a sterile sandwich bag. Seal the bag and label with name, date, and source.
  6. Observe and record the initial status of the slice of bread (consider taking a photograph).
  7. Place the bag in an incubator (25–30 °C) for 48–78 hours.
  8. Following the incubation period, collect the sealed sandwich bag from the incubator. The sealed bag should not be opened under any circumstances.
  9. Observe and record the final status of the slice of bread (consider taking a photograph).
  10. Use a magnifying glass to observe the detail of the micro-organisms growing on the surface of the bread.
💉 Vaccines & Antibiotics
Vaccines
Vaccine
A vaccine is a medical treatment that protects the body from a particular disease. It is made from the pathogen that causes the disease (e.g. the COVID-19 vaccine is manufactured using a small amount of the dead pathogen). This stimulates the immune system to create a response. The body memorises this response in case it comes into contact with the pathogen again in the future.
💡
Eureka – COVID-19 Pandemic
COVID-19 is a respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV-2, first detected in China in late 2019. As it spread globally, most people had no natural immunity, putting the entire global population at risk. Within the first two years, over 450 million cases were reported worldwide. Thanks to remarkable cooperation between medical professionals, scientists and the pharmaceutical industry, vaccines were developed in record time and began rollout from late 2020.
Antibiotics
Antibiotic
An antibiotic is a chemical produced by bacteria and fungi. Antibiotics attack other bacteria and fungi in their environment, which makes them useful in fighting infections caused by these micro-organisms. For example, amoxicillin is used to treat bacterial infections such as chest infections and dental abscesses.
⚠️ Antibiotics have no effect on diseases caused by a virus. Incorrect use of antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance, making it more difficult to treat diseases.

Final Examination 2022 – Q14
Human health is affected by environmental factors such as nutrition. The table compares two similar foods, Food A and Food B (per 80 g serving):

Sugar: A = 18 g, B = 7 g | Saturated fat: A = 7 g, B = 3 g | Cholesterol: A = 55 mg, B = 33 mg | Sodium: A = 330 mg, B = 200 mg | Protein: A = 12 g, B = 20 g

a. Identify one piece of evidence from the table that shows the two foods were compared fairly.
b. Which food, A or B, would be a better choice as part of a healthy diet? Use two pieces of evidence from the table to support your answer.
c. Calculate the percentage protein in Food A.
a. Both foods are compared using the same serving size (80 g per serving). b. Food B is a better choice: - Food B has less sugar (7 g vs 18 g) — reducing risk of type 2 diabetes - Food B has less saturated fat (3 g vs 7 g) — lower risk of heart disease - Food B also has less sodium and cholesterol, and more protein. c. % protein in Food A = (12 g ÷ 80 g) × 100 = 15%
✅ Fair test → same serving size | Better choice → Food B | % protein Food A = 15%
💡 For percentage: always divide the part by the total, then × 100. Protein 12 g ÷ 80 g × 100 = 15%.
